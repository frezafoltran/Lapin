To make a decision, we first calculate the probability of winning a point in each game based on p.
For the first option, the probability of winning a point is given in the question and equals to p.
For the second option, the probability of winning a point equals the probability that you score 2 goals added to probability you score 3 goals. That is, it equals to p^3 + (1-p)*p*p*3 = -2*p^3 + 3*p^2.
We wish to find the values of p for which -2*p^3 + 3*p^2 > p. Or -2*p^3 + 3*p^2 - p > 0. Assuming p != 0, that is equivalent to: -2*p^2 + 3*p - 1 > 0. This has roots at p = 1, 1/2. Thus, for any value of 1 > p > 1/2, the second option is the best option. Else, the first option is best.
